GUINEVERE

Legendary Queen of Britain & Wife of King Arthur

Figure 1: Queen Guinevere, establishing the foundation of Camelot.

Chapter 1: Origins & Marriage

Guinevere is the legendary Queen of Britain and the wife of King Arthur. According to Arthurian legend, she is the daughter of King Leodegrance. When Guinevere married Arthur, her father gifted the young king the mystical Round Table as a wedding present, symbolizing unity and equality among the knights.

Figure 2: The tragic romance between the Queen and Sir Lancelot.

Chapter 2: Love & Betrayal

The great love affair between Guinevere and Sir Lancelot is celebrated yet profoundly tragic. Although Lancelot performs great acts, their love must remain a secret because it constitutes a betrayal of the King. This bitter dissension ultimately leads to a civil war, contributing significantly to the final disasters and the fiery end of Camelot.

Character Analysis: A Complex Queen

In Sir Thomas Malory's Le Morte d'Arthur, Guinevere is portrayed as a multifaceted character. Modern critics often describe her as "jealous and self-willed," reflecting a strong expression of personal desire over loyalty to the King. Unlike traditional aristocratic women, she attempts to take control of her destiny, which creates a deep narrative tension between her royal duties and her emotions as a lover.

Figure 3: The internal tension between royal duty and personal emotion.

"In early 12th-century French works like Chrétien de Troyes's, Guinevere was an idealized heroine of courtly love. However, Malory's 15th-century narrative heavily emphasizes her adultery, guilt, and her share of responsibility for Camelot's downfall."
